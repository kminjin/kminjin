Overview

Context

Our client organization is CNBC and we were tasked with evaluating their year-long redesign of their CNBC iOS app version 5.0.3. The CNBC iOS app has a wide variety of features but after the initial meeting with our client, we narrowed our scope to only evaluating the extensive live TV and stock-charts features as our primary goals and explore gestures and consider general usability issues as our secondary goals.

    • Recommendations at Glance

    01

    Information displayed in stock-charts are important, but the ability to filter out data should not be neglected. Allow manipulation of stock charts(e.g. ability to extend lines through certain points on the graph).

    02

    Consider Youtube as competitor and adopt its gestures. Incorporate double tapping to forward/reverse, and allow users to minimize their video/play in the background while they decide what content they want to watch next.

    03

    CNBC should offer free trials to entice users to download the app and thenoffer more exclusive content so that users feel a need to install the CNBC app as opposed to other applications for finance related content.

    Understand

    Interaction Map

    To better understand our product, we created an interaction map. It allowed us to understand the product from a users' perspective.

    Interviews

    Our most important findings from interviews were:

  • People who preferred watching live TV on mobile applications rather than on TV did so because of less ads and felt content on mobile apps updated faster.
  • Most popular video watching app seemed to be Youtube, and it was because of their recommendation feature, ability to interact with others through likes and comments, and the intuitive gestures such as swiping down to minimize video and being able to multitask.

    We then wanted to understand the people using the CNBC mobile app. To create effective user personas, we decided to interview five students from the Ross School of Business, University of Michiganâ€™s business school. Through the interviews, we collected enough qualitative data to flesh out two types of users.

    Competitive Analysis

    01 Direct

    Bloomberg was a direct competitor because it contained all the main features (markets, news, and video/TV)

    02 Indirect

    WSJ was an indirect competitor because it provided financial news through some other medium than video

    03 Partial

    Marketwatch, IGTV, Youtube Live, Yahoo Finance were all partial competitors because they had only a few overlapping content and features

    04 Parallel

    Robinhood was a parallel competitor because the user base included finance/business interested people

    Surveys

    We then wanted to learn about peopleâ€™s habits and preferences. We were able to collect 43 survey responses from potential users who were students and professors from the Ross School of Business. With the surveys, we were able to learn that people prefer on-demand content much more than live content, half of the survey responses who watch cable TV reported they watch it on their mobile phones, and how people decide to sign up for membership largely depended on the uniqueness of the content more than other factors.

    Heuristics Evaluation

    Most Notable Violations of Heuristics

  • when bookmark option was selected, users were not able to locate where these bookmarked items were being saved
  • the horizontal colored bars across CNBC videos can be an area of confusion since for most video platforms, horizontal bars indicate progress
  • There was also a search icon in the videos section which did not allow for video searching
  • podcasts lack captions or transcript option
  • videos will autoplay when transitioning between vertical view to horizontal view even when paused

    • Usability Testing

    A usability testing was done with five participants to be able to provide concrete quantitative and qualitative data to strengthen our argument for our recommendations. The most crucial urgent recommendations were including subtitles for videos, incorporating gestures into videos that are already prevalent in video platforms like Netflix and Youtube such as double tapping to forward and reverse, and relevant link articles near videos for users who want to learn more beyond watching few minute clips.

    Materialize

    Preference Testing

    We then decided to do a preference testing with eight people to see if people would actually prefer our recommendations over the current design. We cannot show our designs that showcase our recommendations because of the CNBC student project agreement but our designs were preferred 32 out of 32 times.

    Recommendations

    01

    Information displayed in stock-charts are important, but the ability to filter out data should not be neglected. Allow manipulation of stock charts(e.g. ability to extend lines through certain points on the graph).

    02

    Consider Youtube as competitor and adopt its gestures. Youtube was the most mentioned video watching app amongst our interviewees and survey respondents. People mentioned that they liked the gestures Youtube allowed because it limited action time and multitasking. Incorporate double tapping to forward/reverse, and allow users to minimize their video/play in the background while they decide what content they want to watch next.

    03

    CNBC should offer free trials to entice users to download the app and thenoffer more exclusive content so that users feel a need to install the CNBC app as opposed to other applications for finance related content.
