Veg Out

A mobile app that helps people eat less meat, October - Nov 2019

Veg Out was a personal passion product design project that I took on because our planet is deteriorating and I wanted to do something about it. Veg Out is meant to be a one-stop resource for anyone who wants to go meat-free.











My work was important because

01 The animal agriculture industry produces roughly 18% of all produced greenhouse gas emissions (FAOUN, 2006).
02 This industry also utilizes an unsustainable amount of resources. 80-90% of US water consumption is utilized by the animal agriculture industries (USDA, 2016).
03 If everyone reduces their meat consumption, our earth will be healthier!











Solution at Glance



Progress Visualization

People can log a meat-free day and see a visualization of their impact on the world for that week.

Communities

People can join communities and see their ranking compared to others and feel more motivated.

Tools for Success

By providing vegan recipes, Veg Out can become a one stop resource for anyone who wants to go meat-free.

Knowledge

On a weekly basis, the education section is updated with a new informational video and article encouraging an increased meat-free diet.

    Phase 2: Explore

    Developing First Design Concept

    Example pain points in old design & design decisions to address them

    01

    Old Design
    When requesting a security camera,"Site Operations Security Request" seems like a great option (Left Image #2, first View Details box), until you click on the form and realize it's only for location in Zhuhai, China.

    New Design
    Came up with a design that prioritizes location(Right Image #1) first to cut out any unnecessary searching behaviors.


    02

    Old Design
    For an ergonomic furniture request, there is a list of options to select however, not everyone knows what each option means and options that are either/or are checkboxes which allow for multiple selections.

    New Design
    Make the information digestible, offer categories, use radio buttons rather than checkboxes, and offer help where needed with help icons.


    03

    Old Design
    For the overall request selection process, the titles for each request were not clear or sometimes not representative of the request forms. For example, "Badge Reader-Security Lexington" request had more options besides a badge reader, but an employee would not know that from the current design.

    New Design
    Came up with a design that had helper text(right image #3) for each request form




    In the new design, the most complex form can be submitted in four clicks rather than seven.
