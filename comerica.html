Helping Comerica Bank Innovate

UX Research at Comerica Bank, May - Aug 2019

Examined internal communication tools and assessed user needs. Provided solutions within company constraints to increase engagement between our client team and the rest of the employees at Comerica Bank.











Why did Comerica Bank need us?



The clients were dissatisfied with the communication tool they were using to broadcast their news and achievements to the rest of the organization. The clients believed their current communication tool was responsible for the observed low engagement, including low email responses and attendance in all-employee meetings.

We aimed to...

  • Examine how users interacted with the current tool.
  • Understand users' needs.
  • Collect feedback on perception of the Innovation Team from target users.

    • UX Skills Practiced

  • User Interviews
  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Affinity Analysis
  • Survey Design and Analysis

    • Constraints

  • Restricted access to company resources & tools
  • Restricted access to target audience

    • Main Takeaways



  • There are challenges that come with dealing with large corporation infrastructure At school, instructors are supportive and take steps to facilitate the UX research process but UX was not actively and widely championed for in the client company and the culture impeded some efforts.
  • Numbers and statistics are data that everyone can understand because they help people tangibly measure the improvements. If the baseline data is not readily available, it might be worth the effort to gather baseline data before diving into problem solving.

    • What I would have done differently

  • Gather baseline data.
  • Consider limitations and stay away from surveys. I maybe should have participated in observation.

    • Phase 1: Understand

    Background Research

    Looked into their current tools and did a competitive analysis chart on which products overlapped in which features

  • Out of the 8 internal communication tools, 3 tools did not provide different features.

    • Stakeholder Interviews (2)

  • “The expectation going in, and something that might be hard to grasp is that our culture is one where things move at a glacial pace. This permeates through the entire organization and emails go unanswered, projects take far longer than they should”



    • User Needs Assessment

    Interviews (2 from Innovation team, 3 outside of Innovation team)
    Primarily wanted to understand which products people used, which products people did not, and what were their frustration

  • People felt overwhelmed
  • Would receive the same context in multiple application channels and tended to think they already read it somewhere else and delete or not pay attention to them
  • “People who are so focused on transformation can’t see the value of innovation and see it as extra work”
  • Does not remember the last time innovation tried to contact them digitally, only through physical conversations


    Survey
    In hindsight, it was silly to send the survey out when we were trying to figure out a communication problem...

  • Received 3 responses, inconclusive results!
  • People who responded were close to the Innovation team and therefore were biased








    • Conclusions

    Having too many communication tools with overlapping featured led to fragmented and inconsistent outcomes.

  • Not many people remember receiving Innovation Team communications.
  • There are problems with consistency in communication.
  • The large number of communication channels can be harmful.
  • People are not engaged with non-essential content.




    • Solutions

    Because of an agreement, we cannot showcase all our solutions but here is a mockup with dummy information.

  • Recommended tools that had overlapping features to get rid of the number of tools being used.
  • Redesigned their connect page based on user needs.
  • Created a style guide and new logo to better increase their brand identity.
  • Created a new email template to keep communication consistent.
