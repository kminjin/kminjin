    DESIGN PROCESS

    Phase 1 of 4: Problem Framing







    PAIN POINT I

    Employees could not accurately and quickly find the forms they were looking for in ServiceNow.

    DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

    Conduct a card sort and come up with a new information architecture structure that best reflects users' existing mental models.


    PAIN POINT II

    Employees were unsure if the form they were using was correct.

    DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

    Give users context and prevent errors whereever possible. Help build users' confidence by making it clear they are taking the correct or wrong steps.


    PAIN POINT III

    The forms were extensive and pushed users to phone in requests rather than use the existing online system.

    DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

    Make the online system seem less daunting by redesigning all 10 forms. Make them clear and concise.

    BASELINE USABILITY TEST



    Phase 2 of 4: Exploring



    I sent out an online card sort task with the titles of 10 forms to Lexmark employees and personally conducted 10 card sorts with employees. The tests demonstrated that the current organization of content was no where near the results of the card sorts. I also had enough data from the previous baseline usability test to support design decisions.

    NEW INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

    In the previous design, when requesting a security camera, "Site Operations Security Request" seems like a great option, until one clicks on the form and realizes the form is only for Zhuhai, China. The new design prioritizes location first to cut out any unnecessary searching behaviors.


    DESIGN CONCEPT

    01 Forms can be dynamic

    Forms do not always have to display all the information at first load. Only display and ask for information when necessary. Make information appear based on what they select in the forms.

    02 Prevent user error

    Have clear hierarchy and provide categories for the information to keep the forms digestible at a glance. Limit user actions to prevent error (e.g. using radio buttons rather than checkboxes).

    03 Give users more context

    Build users confidence in their decision making processes by providing helper texts and description. Adding more text does not automatically make the design more convoluted if it is done intelligently.

    Phase 4 of 4: Validation



    FINAL USABILITY TEST

    Usability testing of the new design showed a 36% improvement. Like the previous test, we recruited ten employees and each test included ten of the same tasks. After the final round of testing, I made minor changes to strings to clear up some confusion in hopes to raise the 89% success rate before writing the documentation for developers.

    REFLECTION



    When designing a product,experience should come first. Otherwise, be prepared to spend more time and cost compared to initially required amounts later down the road.

    To help users understand where they are and locate what they are searching for, be contextual,transparent, and approachable.


    Always keep in mind that you are designing for people. People prefer simplicity and clarity. Sometimes something simple as a list rather than a description can be clearer.

    Communicate frequently with developers about the specifics.

    hen users are involved in the design process either throug testing or other research methods, results are better.
