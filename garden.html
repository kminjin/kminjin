VR Garden

Virtual Reality App - Google Cardboard

Oct 2019

OVERVIEW

VR Garden is a virtual reality app meant to encourage users to learn about and practice gardening


CONTRIBUTIONS

Gaze/fuse triggered rotating watering jug interaction using A-Frame + Javascript & Unity + C#

TEAM

Kara Dailey, Caitlin Helgesen, Corbet Griffith, Chance Murphy, Adrienne Royce


MOTIVATION

16 million children in America are struggling with hunger and obesity coupled with poor nutrition remains at epidemic proportions



  • Gardening can bring nutritional foods to families living in food deserts.
  • Gardening is cheaper than going to the grocery markets to buy fresh or organic produce.

    • WHY VIRTUAL REALITY?

    A VR experience has the opportunity to breach an informational gap in learning how to garden



  • Provide a low stakes environment to replicate and test variables in a home garden without doing physical damage to actual plants or wallets of its users.
  • The immersive nature of VR will help users consider if gardening can realistically be adopted into their everyday life.

    • IDEATION

    We decided to begin with 360 grid paper templates and modeling clay for physical prototyping



    Main Takeaways

    Included Interactions
  • Gaze/fuse triggered rotating watering can
  • Gaze/fuse triggered shovel interaction
  • Picking of tomatoes

    • Why these interactions?

    Our VR app was meant to be as realistic as possible and we wanted to educate users on how often or how much specific plants need to be watered. We wanted to include a shoveling interaction because preparing soil is also standard for all gardening and we wanted to convey that.






    DIGITAL PROTOTYPING

    Replace users' current realities by increasing immersion: help users feel they are in control, have autonomy, and aid them in establishing a successful presence in the VR world



  • Involve realistic lighting and sounds to entertain users' senses.
  • Make the VR interactions meet user expectations (interactions are predictable and believable).

    • FINAL DELIVERABLE

    Prototyping on A-Frame



    Although I started with Unity, for the final project, we decided to use A-Frame because A-Frame was much more friendly to designers without an intense programming background. For Unity, I had to learn C# for over a week to be able to code a simple interaction, but to protoype in A-Frame, the only required language was html and javascript.


    REFLECTION

    What I learned


    Design for probabilistic areas of user attention based on ergonomic data because designers cannot control what the audience is seeing.


    Ensure that users feel they are in control because VR experiences usually involve a lot of physical movement and therefore stability should be a priority otherwise there can be multiple negative physical reactions such as motion sickness.


    A basic understanding coding languages, especially HTML, CSS, and Javascript is required for AFrame in order to create elements.


    Learning how to position elements on a 3D axis for a virtual reality environment rather than a 2D web page or application is important.
