Process

First Design Problem: How to solve the problem

When taking part in a design project, I believe the first challenge is to steer away from formulaic solutions and predefined methods of the ‚Äústandard design process.‚Äù I spent some time thinking critically about how I wanted to tackle the process. I decided to skip the distribution of surveys and conduct more in depth interviews, and adapt usability tests to ‚Äúmicro-usability tests.‚Äù I knew I wanted to opt out of distributing surveys because it would take time for responses to accumulate and I was more interested in cross questioning, and asking open ended questions rather than closed ended ones.     Competitor: https://events.umich.edu/ on mobile

  • Funded by the University of Michigan
  • Direct Competitor
  • Helps students find events on campus

    • Research Question:

  • What areas of the online resource events.umich.edu on mobile cause users frustration?

    • Participants

  • 2 undergraduate freshmen
  • 2 first year graduate students

    • Tasks

  • You are looking for a community service event for next Friday. Please find an event listing that meets your criteria.
  • In the listing you have selected, can you find the address and the event time?
  • ant to invite me to come along for the event. Can you demonstrate how you would share the event and invite me?

    • Follow Up Questions

  • What are your opinions on the available filtering options?
  • How did you feel about this process?
  • ere any areas of improvement that you had in mind?

    • Findings

  • Users did not like having to scroll down for information such as address and the event time, which they considered the most important.
  • All users did not use the given method of sharing such as through Facebook and Twitter, and rather took screenshots of multiple screens to share with me.
  • Users reported filtering navigation was unclear when first on the home page but became easier after clicking an event.

  • Students desire a one-stop digital resource that provides information about events on campus.
  • Students cannot always comfortably use a laptop so they prefer a mobile resource, whether it‚Äôs a website that is mobile friendly or an app.
  • Students are possibly experiencing alert fatigue. Make sure solution ‚Äúcommunicates without talking‚Äù
  • I decided to design an all events app that also includes orientation events. I didn‚Äôt want to design an app that will only be utilized for several days (orientation).

    User Testing + Revisions #1

    Tasks:

  • Can you find a sports event that is open to undergraduate students and is wheelchair accessible?
  • Can you find events happening on Feb 2nd?
  • How would you reset the filters you have opted for?

    • Findings

  • Users were able to successfully find events they were looking for.
  • I decided to include a clearly displayed ‚Äúfiltering‚Äù drop down menu that overlays over the events. In the interviews, students frequently mentioned they were busy and overwhelmed so they wanted something ‚Äúquick and easy.‚Äù
  • When asked to reset the filtering, some users ran into confusion. For them, the ‚Äúreset‚Äù button wasn‚Äôt intuitive. They also didn‚Äôt realize that once you select filters, the filters in action are displayed on top of the events and you can ‚Äúx‚Äù out of filters displayed but that was also not intuitive.
  • Although in the interviews, students mentioned they were more likely to attend events when their peers had favorable opinions on them, during user testing, I realized students didn‚Äôt actually see a need for reviews even though they like hearing about them.
  • In the filter option overlay, the large buttons as did not allow for many filtering options. I decided to keep it simple without diagrams and just make the options as small boxes you can check and uncheck for the low fidelity wireframe.

    • What I learned about the design process

  • Users might think they know what they dislike and like however, their thoughts do not always translate to their actions. Many students have stated they appreciate opinions of peers when considering which events to go to, but in testing, they thought it was unnecessary.
  • Product design is truly an intersection for all things. Research. Visual. Interaction. Testing.
  • Designing for less is sometimes designing for more. Although I wanted to include options such as ‚Äúsaved searches‚Äù and the ‚Äúnot interested‚Äù options, they only overwhelmed the users.
  • It can be fruitful to step back from the problem. Reading the design challenge prompt, designers might be quick to assume you need to separate categories by five types of events: sports, music, volunteering, visual arts, and social groups. However, there are so many social groups and when I gave myself a day to think about the project, I realized I should also filter out by DIFFERENT social groups.
  • I wish I could have devoted more time to need assessment and personas. I wish I had the time to send out meaningful surveys and get enough responses and spend a day or more on developing personas.

    • What I learned about the design process

  • I work well under pressure. At first I was intimidated by the project deadline since I was doing freelance design work, research, class, etc but it was so much fun! I only began studying UX design in September and in class we are encouraged to spend entire semesters on a project. I really pushed myself for this project and became more confident as a designer so thank you for giving me this project!
  • Users‚Äô wellness are my top priority. Even though I always knew this, this project further confirmed this for me! I decided to stay away from notifications because users are often overwhelmed by notifications and I wanted to set myself apart from solutions that measured engagement as the key metric. I didn‚Äôt want to resort to notifications to bring users back and wanted to measure success of my design by seeing if there is an increase from past years in number of people making it out to events. So instead, I gave users the option to add it to their Google Calendar if they wanted notifications.
