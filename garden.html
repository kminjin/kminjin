Each team member was responsible for a different part of the application. My role is highlighted above. If you want to skip the process, the video of the final prototype can be found here.






PROBLEM STATEMENT

16 million children in America are struggling with hunger and obesity coupled with poor nutrition remains at epidemic proportions (NGA).

  • Gardening can bring nutritional foods to families living in food deserts.
  • Gardening is cheaper than going to the grocery markets to buy fresh or organic produce.







    • WHY VR?

    A virtual reality experience has the opportunity to breach an informational gap in learning how to garden.

  • Provide a low stakes environment to replicate and test variables in a home garden without doing physical damage to actual plants or wallets of its users.
  • The immersive nature of VR will help users consider if gardening can realistically be adopted into their everyday life.







    • IDEATION: PHYSICAL PROTOTYPING

    We decided to begin with 360 grid paper templates and modeling clay for physical prototyping. Sketching and physically prototyping our virtual reality experience helped us narrow our scope by giving us an idea about what parts of the environment and what kinds of interactions will be absolutely necessary to address our problem.


    Main Takeaways

    Included Interactions
  • Gaze/fuse triggered rotating watering can
  • Gaze/fuse triggered shovel interaction
  • Picking of tomatoes

    • Why these interactions?

    Our VR app was meant to be as realistic as possible and we wanted to educate users on how often or how much specific plants need to be watered. We wanted to include a shoveling interaction because preparing soil is also standard for all gardening and we wanted to convey that.






    CONSIDERATIONS FOR DIGITAL PROTOTYPING


    VR experiences aim to replace users' current realities. So Increase immersion by helping users feel they are in control, have autonomy, and aid them in establishing a successful presence in the VR world.

  • Involve realistic lighting and sounds to entertain users' senses.
  • Make the VR interactions meet user expectations (interactions are predictable and believable).








    • DELIVERABLE: VIDEO OF FINAL DIGITAL PROTOTYPE


    Although I started with Unity, for the final project, we decided to use A-Frame because A-Frame was much more friendly to designers without an intense programming background. For Unity, I had to learn C# for over a week to be able to code a simple interaction, but to protoype in A-Frame, the only required language was html and javascript.








    MAIN TAKEAWAYS


    1. Design for probabilistic areas of user attention based on ergonomic data because designers cannot control what the audience is seeing.

    2. Ensure that users feel they are in control because VR experiences usually involve a lot of physical movement and therefore stability should be a priority otherwise there can be multiple negative physical reactions such as motion sickness.

    3. A basic understanding coding languages, especially HTML, CSS, and Javascript is required for AFrame in order to create elements.

    4. Learning how to position elements on a 3D axis for a virtual reality environment rather than a 2D web page or application is important.
