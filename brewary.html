brewARy

Augmented Reality App

Role

AR Designer, Nov 2019

Tools

Unity, Vuforia

Project Context

I created an augmented reality app that can be used during a brewery tour.
My app is capable of two interactions:

  • Recognize a beer can and display information about that beer
  • Recognize people's IDs and calculate if someone is of age to purchase alcohol







    • PROBLEM STATEMENT

    Beer making is an ancient and storied tradition. However in the United States for many decades it was reduced to purchasing Coors and Bud light in 12-packs at the grocery store. Thankfully, the tide has turned and an appreciation for the variety and nuance of the beverage has returned to rival the connoisseurship of wine. The AR app BrewARy is a tool for purveyors of beer to introduce their product and facilite it to consumers.






    WHY AR?

    An augmented reality experience during a brewery tour has the opportunity to provide more ways to interact with the products and setting than a regular tour without the AR app. A visitor's experience is no longer limited to physical interactions but now includes digital ones as well.

  • AR interactions can help skip tedious tasks to achieve a goal because a quick recognition can trigger interactions.
  • An AR experience is never meant to replace a user's reality but rather enhance it.







    • IDEATION: PHYSICAL PROTOTYPING

    We decided to begin with transparencies and templates for physical prototyping. Sketching and physically prototyping our virtual reality experience helped us narrow our scope by giving us an idea about what parts of the environment and what kinds of interactions will be absolutely necessary to address our problem.


    Main Takeaways

    Included Interactions
  • Recognize a beer can and display information about that beer
  • Recognize people's IDs and calculate if someone is of age to purchase alcohol

    • Why these interactions?

    I wanted to decrease the time it takes to search up information about interesting beers online and increase the time spent with the products, the beers, themselves during the tour. Additionally, age is a sensitive topic for many. I wanted to create an AR experience where someone's age did not have to be exposed but a bartender could still make sure that the consumer was of age to purchase alcohol.






    CONSIDERATIONS FOR DIGITAL PROTOTYPING


    AR apps are meant to enhance the experience and not replace it. The app needs to work together with the real-physical environment instead of working independently with no consideration for the environment.

  • Make sure the AR interaction has a reason for it being AR and not the usual 2D interaction.
  • Consider the usefulness of each interaction. Is it just for fun or does the interaction actually have a use?
