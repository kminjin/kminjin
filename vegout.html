Veg Out

A mobile app that helps people eat less meat, October - Nov 2019

Veg Out was a personal passion product design project that I took on because our planet is deteriorating and I wanted to do something about it. Veg Out is meant to be a one-stop resource for anyone who wants to go meat-free.











My work was important because

01 The animal agriculture industry produces roughly 18% of all produced greenhouse gas emissions (FAOUN, 2006).
02 This industry also utilizes an unsustainable amount of resources. 80-90% of US water consumption is utilized by the animal agriculture industries (USDA, 2016).
03 If everyone reduces their meat consumption, our earth will be healthier!











Solution at Glance



Progress Visualization

People can log a meat-free day and see a visualization of their impact on the world for that week.

Communities

People can join communities and see their ranking compared to others and feel more motivated.

Tools for Success

By providing vegan recipes, Veg Out can become a one stop resource for anyone who wants to go meat-free.

Knowledge

On a weekly basis, the education section is updated with a new informational video and article encouraging an increased meat-free diet.

First Design Problem: The Design Process



I spent some time thinking critically about how I wanted to tackle the problem. For this project specifically, I wanted to emphasize people’s narratives and pay close attention to the details of people’s stories so methods I determined to be indispensable were: storyboards, scenarios, personas, and story maps. I also took part in conducting a competitive analysis, spent time ideating and sketching potential solutions. The final prototype was done on Sketch + Invision.

    • Phase 1: Understand

    Competitive Analysis

    I focused on competitors’ strengths, weaknesses, key brand differentiators, competitor type, and examined how they set themselves apart in either their strategy, technology, metrics, design, branding, position, and messaging. The competitors I examined were Oreoco, Gonutts, The Darwin Challenge & Ecoviate.

    Takeaways

  • Numbers are appealing
  • People like seeing progress/results/outputs
  • Positive reinforcement wins over negative reinforcement
  • Community/social aspect are huge motivators
  • Needs to be very well integrated into everyday life without alert fatigue
  • Incentivize, incentivize, incentivize!

    Phase 2: Explore

    Sketching

    I spent significant time ideating and sketching potential solutions. I sketched eight potential solutions and pushing myself to be creative before narrowing it down to one solution for further exploration really helped me expand my boundaries.


    TAKEAWAY: The solution does not need to be a mobile app. However, for the solution I had in mind, a mobile application would be the best choice because it is personal and users can easily access it.





    Paper Prototyping & User Testing


    Findings

  • Although statistics representing benefits was informative, Users also wanted to see a visual representation.
  • The goal setting feature was capped to 15 days and Users were left without choice if they wanted to pursue a longer goal.
  • Users liked how they can choose with benefits would be displayed on their dashboard.
  • Users also reported liking the vegan recipe section because it gave them the tools to succeed.
  • Users also found the dashboard overwhelming and thought the information needed to be split up.
  • Adding a new goal took a lot of steps, and users found it unnecessary.
  • Users also wanted an option to view all benefits instead of being forced to click on each category.

    • TAKEAWAY: Users like choices & do not like having many things on one screen.







    Digital Prototyping + User Testing

    A lot of the usability issues from the paper prototypes were addressed in the first digital prototype. During debriefing, I attempted to get more feedback on the feelings and the product functions. The most common words used to describe my prototype were busy, engaging, responsive, overwhelming, and compelling.
    TAKEAWAY: Although I tried simplifying the dashboard, it was not enough. It needs to be more simple & “sleek.” Usability issues are important but so is the overall feel and vibe of the product. Visual design needs more work.






    Materialize

    Specify Design Context

    After ideating, exploring, and two rounds of user testing, I had a clearer idea on what was NECESSARY for my app to succeed and what other features were overwhelming the user.
    Goal: Design Veg-Out so that it can become a one-stop-app for all needs when attempting to reduce meat-intake.
    Essential Functions: preset & manageable weekly goal

  • positive reinforcement from display of accumulated benefits from users’ number of meat-free days
  • locating communities for social support
  • equipped with vegan meal recipes to go meat-free

    • Human + Social Processes: People like simplicity and connecting with others Ethics of My Design: Functional, convenient, don’t contribute to alert fatigue
