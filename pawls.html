Project Context

Each year, approximately 6.5 million companion animals enter shelters in the US. Less than half of these animals, however, are adopted. In order to address the rampant issue of pet homelessness and increase the likelihood that these animals will have a second chance at life, we decided to explore how ubiquitous computing can help ensure newly adopted pets are remaining in their new homes.
One of the most common behavioral issue that often arises and cause owners to return their new companions is barking. Our product is essentially a smart dog crate, paired with a mobile app, which will be used as a training tool to help dogs decrease their barking.
