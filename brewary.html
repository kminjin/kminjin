brewARy

Augmented Reality App - Google Cardboard

Nov 2019

OVERVIEW

BrewARy is an augmentrd reality app that can enhance people's experience during a brewary tour


CONTRIBUTIONS

Two interactions that can be triggered by beer can and people's ID using Unity + Vuforia

MOTIVATION

There is an increasing trend of appreciation for beer making that is rivaling the connoisseurship of wine



WHY AUGMENTED REALITY?

AR experiences allow for people to be no longer limited to just physical interactions but allows for digital interations as well



  • AR interactions can help skip tedious tasks to achieve a goal because a quick recognition can trigger interactions.
  • An AR experience is never meant to replace a user's reality but rather enhance it.

    • IDEATION

    We decided to begin physical prototyping with transparencies and paper templates



    Main Takeaways

    Included Interactions
  • Recognize a beer can and display information about that beer
  • Recognize people's IDs and calculate if someone is of age to purchase alcohol
    • Why these interactions?

    I wanted to decrease the time it takes to search up information about interesting beers online and increase the time spent with the products, the beers, themselves during the tour. Additionally, age is a sensitive topic for many. I wanted to create an AR experience where someone's age did not have to be exposed but a bartender could still make sure that the consumer was of age to purchase alcohol.






    DIGITAL PROTOTYPING

    AR apps are meant to enhance the experience so make sure the app is working together with the real-physical environment instead of working independently with no consideration for the environment



  • Make sure the AR interaction has a reason for it being AR and not the usual 2D interaction.
  • Consider the usefulness of each interaction. Is it just for fun or does the interaction actually have a use?

    • FINAL DELIVERABLE

    Prototyping with Unity + Vuforia



    Although I started with Unity, for the final project, we decided to use A-Frame because A-Frame was much more friendly to designers without an intense programming background. For Unity, I had to learn C# for over a week to be able to code a simple interaction, but to protoype in A-Frame, the only required language was html and javascript.






    REFLECTION

    What I learned


    AR is meant to work with the environment to provide an experience.


    Make sure the interactions necessitate them being in AR.
