Helping People Become More Meat-Free


Passion Product Design Project

Oct 2019

OVERVIEW

Veg Out is a mobile app that helps people reduce their animal meat intake


CONTRIBUTIONS

  • High-fi prototype on Sketch + Invision
  • Visual design on Adobe Illustrator
  • Storytelling (storyboards, personas, & storymaps)
  • Research (interviews, user tests, & competitive analysis)

    • PROBLEM

    Animal agriculture industries are big contributors to climate change






    They utilize 80-90% of US water consumption.




    They produce ~18% of all greenhouse gas emissions.




    In result, temperature and sea levels are rising.

    SOLUTION

    One-stop mobile app that offers the tools and encouragement users need to reduce meat intake






    FEATURE I
    VISUALIZATION OF PROGRESS

    Users can daily log their meat-free days throughout the week and be encouraged by the visual representation of the environment improving.










    FEATURE II
    CONCRETE METRICS

    Users are able to track their progress and see the positive impact of their accumulated meat-free days.










    FEATURE III
    FRIENDLY MOTIVATION

    Users can form groups with people who have similar goals and compare how they rank to their peers in numnber of meat-free days.










    FEATURE IV
    TOOLS FOR SUCCESS

    Users can look up recipes or learn about vegan diets to equip themselves on this journey.


    PROCESS

    Paying close attention to the details of people's narratives



    COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

    How do other apps try to encourage vegan diets?

    INTERVIEWS

    There are different reasons to why people want to reduce their meat intake




    *10 semi-structured interviews with 3 vegans and 7 nonvegans.

    PERSONAS DERIVED FROM INTERVIEWS

    Target users' backgrounds are diverse but the way they are motivated is similar:

    Users want to be motivated on their meat-free journey by being reminded of their positive impact from eating less meat. Therefore, my solution's first feature became a display of benefits from users' accumulated number of meat-free days.


    STORYMAPS FOR EACH PERSONA

    Become a one-stop resource for this significant life change: vegan recipes

    In each of the written storymaps, users were looking up vegan recipes and therefore access to vegan recipes became the second feature.



    EXPLORING + REFINING

    Focusing on the bare necessities for a successful product



    SKETCHING

    A mobile application would be the best medium for the solution because it is portable, easy to access, and the solution's success depends on daily logging.










    PAPER PROTOTYPES

    Lo-fi prototypes were useful because they can be done in a short amount of time and be iterated on quickly for user testing.











    FIRST DIGITAL PROTOTYPE

    With each iteration of user testing, I was able to eliminate features that I thought would be useful but users found overwhelming. For example, I replaced graphs with understandable-at-a-glance animation.


    SOLUTION

    One-stop mobile app that offers the tools and encouragement users need to reduce meat intake






    REFLECTION

    What I learned


    Technology only amplifies underlying human forces. For technology to have positive impact, the right intent and capacity have to be there first.

    When designing for an app that is meant to encourage a lifestyle change, it does not mean include many features. Try only designing for the bare necessity for products to succeed.



    Where to improve


    Push myself further in visual design by thinking about the affordances on and transitions between the screens.
